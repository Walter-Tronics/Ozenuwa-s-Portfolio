What is an NFT?
Non-fungible tokens, or NFT, for short are unique digital items that are distributed on a blockchain. These digital items are different from one another by a unique number. NFT, meaning non-fungible token, is a digital asset that represents real-world like art, music, in-game, video, and even real estate, which can be otherwise referred to as a “cryptographic asset” having a unique identification code and metadata that differentiates it from a fungible token. Generally, they are encoded with the same underlying software as many cryptos, however, as with cryptocurrency, they can not be exchanged or traded at equivalent values. The ownership of an NFT is recorded in a blockchain, a form of distributed ledger, where transfers take place between the owner and the buyer, allowing NFT to be sold and traded.
